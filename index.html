I burried you in the past, yet I still find myself at your grave.



I told myself it was over-that the final goodbye had been said, that the earth had swallowed what was left of you and of us. I laid our memories down like wilting flowers, one by one, thinking time would decay them into something unrecognizabe, something easier to forget. But some ghost do not rest.


Some wounds do not scab.


I left, yes. But not because I stop caring. I still check up on you-quietly, from afar, like a shadow that never quite fades. I needed to let go, not because it didn't matter, but because it mattered too much. I walked away for the freedom of us both, hoping you'd breathe easier without the weight of us dragging you down.


And yet, here I am again. Kneeling at the place where I thought I buried it all only to realize I never truly did. Because leaving doesn't mean I never loved you, never stayed up hoping you'd be okay. It only means I choose peace over pain, growth over grasping.


Graves are strange places. Not just for the dead, but for the love we had to put down before it turned cruel. And still, I find myself here-not to rekindle, not to reopen, but simply to remember. To care, quietly. Still.
